❓ HOW TO USE

▶ show

1. Pick your class. The bar at the top sets your class, which loads the relevant gear from the wiki and pre-fills stat weights tuned for that class. Weights can be adjusted freely — drag any slider to tell the optimizer what you care about.

2. Find your best loadout. Set a Max Level and quality tier if you want, then hit Find Best Loadout. The Loadout Builder on the right will fill every slot with the highest-scoring item available. Click any slot to swap an item manually, lock slots you want to keep, and hit the button again to fill the rest.

3. Track your current gear. Fill in the Current Gear panel on the left with what you're actually wearing. Once both panels have items, a Loadout Comparison bar appears below showing the score difference and percentage upgrade between what you have and what the optimizer suggests.

4. Add missing items. Don't see something? Use the Add Custom Item panel — enter them manually. Custom items are included in all future optimizations.

📋 NOTES & OMISSIONS

▶ show

How Gear Score Works — Each item is assigned a score based on its stats and your current stat weight sliders. The math is straightforward: for every stat on an item, its value is multiplied by the corresponding weight you've set (0–10), and all of those products are added together. An item with STR 20 is worth twice as much as one with STR 10, assuming your STR weight is the same. This includes all direct stats — STR through RES and resist stats (MR/ER/PR/VR). Items with worn, aura, or proc spell effects also contribute: haste is scored using the Haste weight slider, and any stats granted by worn or aura spells (such as STR or AGI) are scored the same way as direct stats. Proc-effect values are discounted by 50% to account for uptime. The Spell & Passive Effects panel below the loadout shows a full breakdown of all active effects. The optimizer then picks the highest-scoring item available for each slot given your level filter and quality tier. Blessed items multiply all stats by ×1.5 (rounded) and add +1 Resonance. Godly items multiply all stats by ×2 and add +2 Resonance. Adjusting the stat weight sliders is the main way to tailor the optimizer to your build — prioritize what matters most to your class and playstyle, and the scores will shift accordingly.

Charms — Charms have been intentionally omitted from this optimizer. Unlike other gear slots, charms modify character proficiencies rather than base stats directly, and their effects are not represented in the game's data files in a way that maps cleanly to stat weights. Please consult the Erenshor wiki for more information about charms and how to choose the right one for your build.
